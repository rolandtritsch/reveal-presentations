BLE - Bluetooth Low Energy

  • RFID vs. NFC vs. BLE
  • Apple iBeacons
  • Requires 4.3 (API Level 19)
  • BLE Frameworks
    • LocalSocial, Gimbal, RadiusNetworks, Estimote, ...
BLE is not RFID and it's not NFC. One of the reasons it is popular and hyped is, because Apple branded BLE beacons as iBeacons (i.e. they standardized the scanrecord and the configuraation (e.g. the duty cycle) for the beacons) and have announced that they are going to put beacons into every Apple Store (to drive things like contactsless self-checkout)).
Apple is leading the charge. On iOS support for BLE/iBeacons is very good. Android needs to catch up and it does. But ... you need at least API level 19.
Besides the build in support on the Android platform, there are frameworks that aim to give you a higher-level interface to the concept of proximity and nearness and also try to give you a cross-platform experience (for iOS and Android) that is based on the same concepts and abstractions.

Akka - I am glad you asked ...

  • Implementation of Erlang actors
  • Nice/best way to conceptionalize concurrency
  • Spray, Play, Gatling, ...
  • Does it work? - macroid-akka-pingpong
Now ... next/last question: Does Akka work on Android? Short answer is, yes - it does!!!
If you do not know Akka, it is an implementation of the Erlang actor/messaging model, which allows you to build/embrace concurrency in a very nice/safe way.
